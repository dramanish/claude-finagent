# AU QSR Sector

## Market Size
- Total AU QSR market: USD 5.5B (2025) → USD 7.7B by 2034 (CAGR 3.72%)
- QSR = 43.2% of total AU foodservice — largest segment
- AU donut market: AUD ~$329M (2025), CAGR 4.80% to 2035

## Store Growth
- 2025: 359 openings, 109 closures → **net +250 stores** (strongest net growth on record)
- Category expanding despite cost headwinds

## Cost Pressures
- Labour ~40% of operating costs; wage growth outpacing productivity (~1.1%)
- Rising rents squeezing single-location operators
- Structural advantage to scaled franchise networks over independents

## Consumer Trends
- Foot traffic +14% in 2023 driven by value promotions and digital loyalty
- Takeaway fastest growing (11.89% CAGR); dine-in slowing (46% share)
- Health-conscious pressure on gourmet/premium — some closures (Mr Donut Melbourne, Donut Papi Sydney)
- Value positioning resilient; premium artisan more exposed

## Channel Shifts
- Delivery (Uber Eats, DoorDash): meaningful volume but margin-dilutive
- Convenience retail (petrol, supermarket): emerging battleground — see [[Krispy Kreme AU]]/Ampol
- Occasions/catering (high ATV, low footprint): growing — see [[Donut King]] Occasions (3.8× ATV)
- Digital ordering: strong category-wide adoption

## Key Players
- [[Daniels Donuts]] — LKG portfolio company, 50+ VIC/NSW stores
- [[Krispy Kreme AU]] — channel expansion play via QSR and petrol partnerships
- [[Donut King]] — 203 outlets, digital leader, owned by RFG (ASX)
- [[Collins Foods]] — KFC AU operator, best listed bellwether for value-consumer health
- [[Brooklyn Donuts]] — 20 stores, specialty/franchise, national expansion ambition
